Set - Re's Right-Hand Man

Son of Nut, Chosen of Re, Lord of the Desert: Set - the embodiment of chaos, strength, and protection. Revered and feared, he stood as a complex figure—a defender of Re against cosmic threats and a symbol of untamed power. Explore the intricate duality of this enigmatic deity and his role in the divine order.

Important Dates and Festivals for 2025

17TH JANUARY

𓃩

Procession of Set and His Followers

The day of the going forth of Set with his followers to the eastern horizon, and the navigation of Ma’at to the place where the gods are.

28TH JANUARY

𓃩

“The Day Set Kills the Rebel”

“Do not go out today at the beginning of dawn. It is the day of seeing the rebel, and killing him by Set, at the prow of the great barque.”

1 - 7TH FEBRUARY

𓃩

Festival of thunder and lightning

A vibrant seven-day celebration believed to honor the god of storms.

1ST APRIL

𓃩

Procession of Set, Son of Nut

“Going forth of Set, son of Nut, to disturb the great ones who hold him back in his town of Su (an unknown town in the Herakleopolite nome). Then these gods recognized him, and they repelled his followers. None of them remained.”

9-10TH APRIL

𓃩

Festival of Seth, Lord of the Oasis

This same festival may have been carried out in the Kharga Oasis (Osing 1995, 233), and was observed in multiple oases through the Roman period, despite the discontinuation of Set-related cultus in other parts of Egypt, and in royal religious ritual, past the New Kingdom (Kaper 1997, 233ff) (Feast of Set, Lord of the Oasis)

4TH JUNE

𓃩

Day of the Living Children of Nut

“It is the day of the living great ones, the children of Nut (Osiris, Haroeris, Set, Isis, and Nephthys). Do not go out on it until daybreak.”

15TH JULY

𓃩

Feast of Set

The Two-Day Festival of Set in ancient Egypt honored Set’s dual nature as both a protector against chaos and a symbol of destructive forces. Celebrated with offerings, mythological reenactments, and possibly public feasting, the festival emphasized Set’s role in defending cosmic order and legitimizing the pharaoh’s strength.

17TH JULY

𓃩

Accession of Sutekh

Particularly emphasizing his strength, sovereignty, and role in the divine order. Likely associated with rituals of kingship and renewal.

15TH AUGUST

𓃩

Set's Birthday

Celebrated on the third day of the Epagomenal Days as part of the annual five-day period that fell just before the start of the new year.

31ST AUGUST

𓃩

Feast of the Meskhetiu constellation

Meskhetiu is the ancient Egyptian name for the constellation known to us today as Ursa Major, the Great Bear. It is a symbol and aspect of Set

1ST SEPTEMBER

𓃩

The Day of Unknown Rage in the Duat

Refers to the fighting between Harsiese and Set for the throne of Egypt.

2ND SEPTEMBER

𓃩

Feast of Sebaka

“Favorable to do anything on it.” Sebaka, the ancient Egyptian name for the planet Mercury, is a symbol of the god Set.

13TH SEPTEMBER

𓃩

Peace between Horus and Set

“Peace on the part of Horus with Seth. Do not kill any ankhut-snake today. Make a holiday.”

16TH OCTOBER

𓃩

Feast of Harsiese and Set

This festival emphasized the mythological struggle between Set and Horus, as well as the eventual reconciliation and unity between the two gods, symbolizing the balance between chaos and order.

11NT NOVEMBER

𓃩

Reconciliation, judged by Thoth

Judged by the god Thoth, the festival commemorated Thoth’s role in mediating and restoring harmony between Set and Horus after their prolonged struggle for the throne.

24TH NOVEMBER

𓃩

Killing the Enemy

“The day Thoth acted. Speech by the Majesty of Ra in the presence of the great ones. Afterward, these gods together with Thoth caused the enemy of Set to kill himself in his Set’s sanctuary. This was done by the executioners of Qosret.” says Kosar, a form of Set as a Syrian war god.
