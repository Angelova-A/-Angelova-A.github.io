Apollon - Shooter from Afar

The Lyre and The Bow

Apollo acquired the lyre as a gift from Hermes, who crafted it from a tortoise shell after stealing Apollo's cattle. In exchange for forgiveness, Hermes presented the lyre, and Apollo, entranced by its music, accepted it alongside Hermes' clever wit. The bow, however, was a gift from Hephaestus, forged in the fires of Olympus to aid Apollo in defending the divine order.

The Oracle

As the god of prophecy, Apollo claimed the Oracle of Delphi by slaying the great serpent Python, which had long guarded the sacred site. By purging the land of its malevolent influence, Apollo sanctified the oracle as a place of divine prophecy, making it the most revered center of guidance in the ancient world.

The Sun

Apollo became associated with the sun after Helios, the original sun god, passed the duty of guiding the fiery chariot across the heavens. The transition marked Apollo as the light-bringer, illuminating the earth each day with his radiant power and linking him forever to the cycle of dawn and dusk.

Calendar

7TH OF EVERY MONTH

Apollo's Holly Day

In ancient Greek tradition, the 7th of each month was sacred to Apollo, honoring his birth and divine radiance.

20TH FEBRUARY

The Thæophánia

The Thæophánia (Theophania) was a sacred festival in ancient Greece celebrating the epiphany or divine manifestation of Apollo, traditionally linked to his return to Delphi in the spring.

5TH APRIL

Delphinia

Delphinia was an ancient Greek festival held in honor of Apollo Delphinius, celebrating his role as a protector of ships and navigation. Observed in the spring, it featured processions and offerings, including olive branches, symbolizing peace and the god’s guidance over journeys and transitions.

4TH + 5TH MAY

Thargelia

The Thargelia, held in late spring, honored Apollo and Artemis on their birthdays (the 7th and 6th day of the month respectively).

10TH MAY

Libation to Apollon

A libation was a sacred act of devotion, performed to a different deity each month, often involving the pouring of wine, water, honey, or oil onto an altar or the ground.

1ST - 9TH AUGUST

Carneia

Major Spartan festival dedicated to Apollo Carneios, celebrated in late summer to honor the god as a protector of herds and a bringer of order. The festival featured military-style processions, choral performances, and rituals emphasizing discipline, unity, and the harmony of the community under Apollo’s guidance.
